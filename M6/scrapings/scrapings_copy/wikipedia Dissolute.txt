Russian: ро́спуск (ru) m (róspusk) (by an outside agent), распа́д (ru) m (raspád) (by itself)
The quality of being dissolute.Synonym: dissipationHe led a life of dissolution, drinking and gambling almost daily.
The termination of an organized body or legislative assembly, especially a formal dismissal.Synonyms: abolition, disestablishmentAntonyms: establishment, foundation
Japanese: 解体 (ja) (kaitai), 解散 (ja) (kaisan), 廃止 (ja) (haishi)
From Middle English dissolucioun, from Old French dissolucion, from Latin dissolūtiō (“a dissolving, destroying, breaking up, dissolution”).
