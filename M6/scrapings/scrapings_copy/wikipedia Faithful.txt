The film was received poorly at the box office, grossing just $2,104,439. It was a huge drop compared to Cher's previous starring role, Mermaids, which grossed $35,419,397 in 1990. The film's opening weekend of just $967,956 would end as 46% of the film's total gross.[citation needed] This film was Cher's least successful film of the decade in a starring role.
Faithful is a 1996 American comedy crime drama film directed by Paul Mazurskyand starring Cher, Chazz Palminteri and Ryan O'Neal. Palminteri wrote the screenplay, which is an adaptation of his stage play of the same name. Faithful tells the story of a woman, her husband and a hit man. The film was entered into the 46th Berlin International Film Festival.[2] This is Mazursky's final theatrical film as director.
